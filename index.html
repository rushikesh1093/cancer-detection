Leukemia Overview

Leukemia: A Brief Overview

Leukemia is a type of blood cancer that affects the white blood cells. It originates in the bone marrow, where abnormal cells multiply uncontrollably, disrupting the production of healthy blood cells. Symptoms may include fatigue, easy bruising or bleeding, frequent infections, and swollen lymph nodes. Treatment typically involves chemotherapy, radiation therapy, targeted therapy, or stem cell transplantation.
Diagnosis of leukemia typically involves blood tests, bone marrow biopsy, and imaging tests. Once diagnosed, treatment options may include chemotherapy, radiation therapy, targeted therapy, immunotherapy, or stem cell transplantation.

Preventative Measures Against Cancer

Reduce your risk of cancer by adopting healthy lifestyle habits. Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits and vegetables, maintain a healthy weight, exercise regularly, avoid tobacco and excessive alcohol consumption, and protect yourself from the sun. Regular screenings and self-examinations can also help detect cancer early when treatment is most effective.
